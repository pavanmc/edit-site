    • 1. Purpose Of Life

    To achieve by -

    1) Dharma : living or behaving in accordance with moral and ethical principles.

    2) Artha : making a living through activities that are allowed and approved by the law.

    3) Kama : satisfying or achieving something that we strongly want or wish for

    4) Moksha : to attaining a state of spiritual redemption, often associated with religious or philosophical beliefs

    2. Branches Of Ayurveda

    They are 8 Branches of Treatment (Chikitsa) -

    1) Kaya Chikitsa : General Medicine - the branch of healthcare that deals with the prevention, diagnosis, and non-surgical treatment of various illnesses and health conditions.

    2) Bala Chikitsa : Paediatrics - the branch of medicine that specializes in the healthcare of infants, children, and adolescents.

    3) Graha Chikitsa : Psychiatry - the branch of medicine that deals with the study, diagnosis, and treatment of mental illnesses, emotional disorders, and behavioral problems.

    4) Urdhvanga Chikitsa : Disease & treatment of Ear, Nose, Throat, Eyes, & Head (above the neck)

    5) Shalya Chikitsa : Surgery - it specialty that involves the use of manual or instrumental techniques to treat diseases, injuries, or deformities through operative procedures.

    6) Damshrta Chikitsa : Toxicology - the study of poisons or toxins and their effects on living organisms.

    7) Jara Chikitsa : Geriatrics - the branch of medicine that focuses on the health and care of older adults.

    8) Vrushya Chikitsa : Aphrodisiac therapy - it refers to the use of substances or treatments with the intention of enhancing sexual desire, arousal, or pleasure.

    3. Tridosha Of Ayurveda

    Tridosha means 3 Dosha, And Dosha means that which can cause problems, they are -

    1) Vata : Air, Vayu - Below umbilicus part is dominated by Vata.

    2) Pitta : Fire, Heat - Between chest and umbilicus is dominated by Pitta.

    3) Kapha : Cold - The top part upto chest is dominated by Kapha.

    Perfect balance of three Doshas leads to health, imbalance in Tridosha leads to diseases.

    4. Types of digestive fires Of Ayurveda

    They are 4 types of digestive fires (Agni) -

    1) Vishama Agni :

    - Influence : Influenced by Vata dosha.

    - Characteristics : Digestive fire is irregular; the person may experience fluctuations in appetite, with periods of both high and low appetite.

    2) Teekshna Agni :

    - Influence : Influenced by Pitta dosha.

    - Characteristics : Digestive power is high; the person typically has a strong appetite and efficient digestion.

    3) Manda Agni :

    - Influence : Influenced by Kapha dosha.

    - Characteristics : Digestive power is low; the person tends to have a sluggish digestion and may experience a reduced appetite.

    4) Sama Agni :

    - Influence : Influenced by a perfect balance of all three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha).

    - Characteristics : Digestive fire is in harmony; the person enjoys proper appetite and digestion, occurring at appropriate times.

    In Ayurveda, maintaining a balanced Agni is considered crucial for overall health. The different types of Agni highlight how the balance or imbalance of the three doshas can influence digestive function, appetite, and overall well-being.
